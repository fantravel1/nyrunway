Elena Vasquez

Emerging • Brooklyn

Blending Dominican heritage with downtown minimalism, Elena Vasquez has built a cult following through her architectural draping and hand-dyed organic silks. A graduate of Parsons, her collections explore the intersection of Caribbean textile traditions and contemporary New York restraint, earning her a spot on the CFDA emerging talent watchlist.

Marcus Chen-Williams

Established • Midtown

Three-time CFDA nominee and a fixture of the New York Fashion Week calendar since 2014. His label redefines American tailoring by weaving together Chinese textile traditions and Savile Row precision, producing suits and structured pieces that have been worn on red carpets from the Met Gala to the Cannes Film Festival.

Priya Okonkwo

Underground • Bushwick

Nigerian-Indian heritage, zero-waste philosophy, maximum visual impact. Priya creates limited-run collections using deadstock fabrics sourced from Garment District surplus bins. Every piece is numbered and comes with a provenance card tracing its materials. She shows exclusively at off-calendar guerrilla presentations in Brooklyn warehouses.

James Kowalski

Streetwear • Lower East Side

Born and raised on Orchard Street, Kowalski bridges skate culture and couture construction with a raw authenticity that earned him features in both Vogue and Thrasher in the same year. His signature oversized silhouettes with hidden tailoring details have redefined what streetwear can be in a post-hype era.

Amara St. Claire

Sustainable • Williamsburg

Former environmental scientist turned fashion designer, Amara St. Claire operates a closed-loop atelier in Williamsburg where every garment is crafted from plant-dyed, organically grown fibers. Her label holds B Corp certification and partners with regenerative farms upstate, proving that luxury and ecological responsibility are not mutually exclusive.

Nikolai Petrov

Luxury • Upper East Side

Trained at the Chambre Syndicale in Paris before relocating to New York, Nikolai Petrov brings old-world couture techniques to an American luxury context. His atelier on Madison Avenue produces fewer than 200 made-to-measure pieces per year, each requiring over 400 hours of hand-stitching. His clientele includes heads of state and museum patrons.

Suki Yamamoto-Reyes

Emerging • SoHo

Japanese-Puerto Rican designer whose debut collection at NYFW Spring 2025 received a standing ovation. Suki merges the precision of Japanese pattern-making with the vibrant colorwork of Caribbean textiles, creating pieces that feel simultaneously ancient and futuristic. Named one of Business of Fashion's 500 most influential people in fashion.

David Osei

Student • Harlem

A senior at FIT with a thesis collection that went viral before it ever hit a runway. David draws on Ghanaian kente weaving traditions and Harlem ballroom culture to create gender-fluid eveningwear that challenges every convention of formal dress. He was awarded the Geoffrey Beene Design Scholar Award and already has orders from three major retailers.

Elena Vasquez: Building a Bridge Between Santo Domingo and Downtown Brooklyn

When Elena Vasquez arrived in New York at seventeen with a suitcase full of hand-embroidered fabric from her grandmother's workshop in Santo Domingo, she had no connections to the fashion industry, no formal training, and no plan beyond survival. Fourteen years later, her label — VASQ — is carried by Bergdorf Goodman and Ssense, her Spring 2025 collection was reviewed by every major fashion publication on the planet, and she has become one of the most closely watched emerging designers in America.

But the story of Elena Vasquez is not a simple rags-to-runways narrative. It is a story about what happens when two textile traditions — the centuries-old embroidery techniques of the Dominican Republic and the clean-line minimalism of New York modernism — collide in the hands of someone who refuses to choose between them. Her atelier in Gowanus occupies a converted auto-body shop where six artisans, three of them recruited from her grandmother's village, work alongside two Parsons-trained pattern makers. The result is clothing that carries the weight of heritage without the burden of nostalgia — pieces that feel rooted in a specific cultural lineage yet entirely of this moment.

About the Designer Index

The NYRunway Designer Index is the most comprehensive encyclopedia of New York-based fashion designers ever assembled. It catalogs over 1,200 designers across every category — established houses, emerging talents, underground studios, streetwear labels, sustainable brands, luxury ateliers, and student designers. Each profile includes the designer's biography, founding story, aesthetic philosophy, borough roots, complete collection archive, awards history, press coverage, and an interactive career timeline. The Index is searchable by name, neighborhood, category, aesthetic keywords, and season of debut.

Inclusion in the Designer Index is based on a designer's demonstrable connection to New York City — either through a studio or atelier based in one of the five boroughs, regular participation in New York Fashion Week or off-calendar presentations within the city, or graduation from a New York-based fashion institution with an active practice. We do not require CFDA membership, commercial success, or a minimum number of seasons shown. Our goal is to document the full ecosystem — from established names with decades of runway history to student designers showing their thesis collections for the first time. Nominations are accepted year-round through our submission form.

The Designer Index is a living document, updated on a continuous editorial cycle. New designer profiles are added weekly. Existing profiles are updated each season with new collection documentation, press reviews, awards, and career milestones. Major updates coincide with the biannual NYFW calendar — in February and September — when dozens of new profiles and collection archives are published simultaneously. Our editorial team also monitors off-calendar shows, pop-up presentations, and independent showcases throughout the year to ensure emerging and underground designers receive timely coverage.
